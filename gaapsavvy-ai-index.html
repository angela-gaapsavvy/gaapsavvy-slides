Where We Stand

AI in Accounting — From the Ground Up

We started GAAPSavvy with a simple observation: the people doing the work weren't in the room when AI decisions were being made. Vendors were selling. Consultants were advising. But practitioners — the ones reconciling, reviewing contracts, closing the books — were figuring it out alone.

So we built a space for them. And after six AI Shareforums, 650+ conversations, and 30+ companies sharing openly, here's what we know to be true:

🧭
Practitioners should lead this.
The best AI adoption happens when the people closest to the work decide what to automate, what to question, and what to keep human. Not a vendor. Not a headline. You.
🌱
Small wins are real wins.
You don't need a budget, a strategy deck, or permission from innovation. A memo drafted in Claude. A formula written by ChatGPT. A training video made over lunch. That's where this starts — and it compounds.
🪞
Honesty over hype.
We track what's working and what isn't. When a tool falls short, we say so. When something fails, we share that too. The index below isn't marketing — it's what our community actually reported.
Mid-2025
First AI Shareforum. Most teams were curious but hadn't started. The energy was cautious — and electric.
Late 2025
Real use cases started flowing in. Contract review, close management, and AI-written scripts emerged as the breakout categories.
Early 2026
Teams are connecting AI to ERPs, building custom agents, and comparing alternatives. Budget-friendly tools challenge incumbents. SOX and controls framework becomes the top question.
March 2026
Claude Cowork and finance plugins arrive — enabling non-coders to build flux analysis, analytical procedures, and income statement automation in under an hour. Agentic AI becomes top priority (Dropbox). API security governance (Workato) and auditor readiness emerge as critical concerns. Build AND buy replaces build vs. buy.

What Functions Are Getting Automated First?

Where practitioners are actually applying AI — ranked by reported adoption across the community.

AI Application by Accounting Function
Self-reported usage · Q4 2025 – Q1 2026 · n=30+ companies · 6 sessions
Research & writing is the universal entry point Every practitioner we spoke with, regardless of maturity level, uses AI for research, drafting, and communication. It's the lowest-friction starting point.
Contract review is the hottest — and most competitive — use case ASC 606 / MSA review came up in all 6 sessions. The landscape is shifting: Dimely and Luminance emerge as budget-friendly alternatives to Klarity. Glean agents being built at multiple companies. Agentic AI for GL (JE, recs, taxes) is the next frontier.
ERP + AI integration is the new frontier Benchling connected Claude to NetSuite, Box, and Slack — pulling cross-system context for flux analysis and disclosures. Numeric's AI-native "memory" feature layers prior-year context automatically. The gap: controls and SOX frameworks haven't caught up yet.
Automation scripting is the surprise skill Apps Script and Office Script — AI-written, no-code-experience required — emerged as the biggest efficiency win in multiple sessions.
